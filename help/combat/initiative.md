combat initiative  -- How initiative scores are determined and what
                      affects them.

Formula:
  Initiative = 1d10 + Dexterity + Composure + weapon Initiative modifier

  The weapon modifier is listed in the weapon's profile and is usually
  negative (e.g., a sword is -2, a rifle is -4). Drop the weapon or sling
  it to remove its penalty. Drawing or picking up a weapon costs an instant
  action and may negate Defense for that turn.

  Wielding two weapons: apply the larger penalty, then subtract 1 more.
  Example: crowbar (-2) + riot shield (-4) = -5 total.

Ambush (before initiative is rolled):
  Attacker rolls Dexterity + Stealth.
  Defender rolls Wits + Composure.
  If the defender fails, she cannot act or apply Defense on the first turn.
  Initiative is determined normally starting from the second turn.

Ties:
  Break ties by Composure first, then Dexterity. If still tied, the
  Storyteller decides or players may roll off.

Defense degradation:
  Each time a character applies Defense against an attack in a turn,
  his Defense drops by 1 for subsequent attacks until his next turn.
  A character may voluntarily skip applying Defense against an attack
  to preserve it for a later, more dangerous one.
  Spending 1 Willpower adds +2 Defense against one attacker for that
  exchange only.

Delaying your action:
  Declare delay on your turn. You can interrupt at any point later in
  the round. Your new slot becomes your fixed place for subsequent rounds.
  If the round ends before you act, you lose the action for that round.
