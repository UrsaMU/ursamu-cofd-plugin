condition mechanics  -- Conditions vs Tilts, persistence, Beat economy.

Conditions vs Tilts:
  Conditions are narrative-mechanical states that persist beyond the
  current scene and award Beats on resolution.

  Tilts are scene-bound combat or environment effects and award no
  Beats. The +condition/list output marks them as Tilt-P (Personal)
  or Tilt-E (Environmental).

Persistence:
  A leading '*' in /list output marks Persistent Conditions: those
  that endure across many scenes and require sustained effort or
  in-fiction events to resolve (Addicted, Broken, etc).

  Non-Persistent Conditions usually resolve within a session.

Catalog key format:
  Lowercase kebab slugs. Compound names hyphenate:
    shaken
    embarrassing-secret
    shadow-soul
    knocked-down

Beat economy:
  Conditions award 1 Beat on resolution by default (a few award more;
  see /list output). Beats convert to Experience at 5:1; see +xp.

  Tilts award 0 Beats. They exist to track scene-state, not to drive
  advancement.

  Notes attached via /<note> are display-only and do not affect Beat
  awards.
